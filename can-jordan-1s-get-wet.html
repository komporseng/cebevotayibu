Can Jordan 1s get wet?

When it comes to sneaker care, one of the common concerns among sneaker enthusiasts is whether their beloved Jordan 1s can withstand exposure to water.

Jordan 1s are iconic and stylish sneakers that are popular for their timeless design and cultural significance, but their construction and materials can raise questions about their ability to handle wet conditions. 

Understanding the Construction of Jordan 1s

Jordan 1s are typically constructed with a combination of leather, synthetic materials, and rubber outsoles. While the leather upper is durable and long-lasting, exposure to water can potentially damage it. 

The midsole and outsole materials can be affected by prolonged exposure to moisture. The stitching and adhesive used in the construction of the sneakers can also be compromised when wet.

So, it's important to consider these factors when evaluating the sneakers' ability to withstand water.

Can Jordan 1s Handle Water?

While Jordan 1s are not designed to be waterproof, they can handle light exposure to water.

A brief encounter with rain or a few splashes should not cause significant damage to the sneakers if they are promptly dried. 

But prolonged exposure to water, such as submerging them in deep puddles or wearing them in heavy rain for an extended period, can lead to damage and discoloration of the materials.

Protecting Jordan 1s from Water Damage

To protect your Jordan 1s from water damage, consider the following measures:

  • Waterproofing Spray: Applying a waterproofing spray specifically designed for sneakers can create a protective barrier against water. Be sure to choose a product that is suitable for the materials used in Jordan 1s, and follow the application instructions carefully.

  • Avoiding Wet Conditions: Whenever possible, it's best to avoid wearing Jordan 1s in wet or rainy conditions. Opt for alternative footwear if you expect inclement weather.

  • Cleaning and Drying: If your Jordan 1s do get wet, gently remove any excess moisture with a clean cloth and allow them to air dry at room temperature. Avoid using direct heat sources, as they can damage the materials.

Cleaning Wet Jordan 1s

In the event that your Jordan 1s do get wet, it's essential to clean them properly to prevent lasting damage. Here's a step-by-step guide to cleaning wet Jordan 1s:

  • Remove Excess Moisture: Use a clean, dry cloth to gently blot away any excess water from the surface of the sneakers.

  • Air Dry: Allow the sneakers to air dry at room temperature. Stuff them with paper towels or a shoe tree to help maintain their shape as they dry.

  • Cleaning the Upper: Once the sneakers are dry, use a soft brush or cloth to gently remove any dirt or stains from the leather upper. Be gentle to avoid damaging the material.

  • Cleaning the Midsole and Outsole: For the midsole and outsole, use a mild detergent or specialized sneaker cleaner and a soft brush to gently scrub away dirt and grime. Rinse with a damp cloth and allow to air dry.

  • Conditioning the Leather: After cleaning, consider applying a leather conditioner to the upper to keep the leather supple and prevent it from drying out.

    • While Jordan 1s are not completely waterproof, they can withstand light exposure to water with proper care and maintenance.

     Remember to stay mindful of the weather conditions and take the necessary precautions to protect your sneakers from water damage.
