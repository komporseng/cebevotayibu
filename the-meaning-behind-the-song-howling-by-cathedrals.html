The Meaning Behind The Song: Howling by Cathedrals

When listening to a song, it is often the lyrics that captivate us and make us ponder about their meaning. One such song that has intrigued many is “Howling” by Cathedrals. Although not much information is available about the song, the lyrics themselves offer a glimpse into the emotions and experiences the artist wishes to convey.

The opening lines of the song, “Hot night coming, keep the car running, lavender fingers, swallow my pollen,” create a vivid image of a passionate and intense encounter. The words “hot night” and “lavender fingers” invoke a sense of heat and sensuality, while “swallow my pollen” adds a touch of intimacy and vulnerability. The chorus then reveals the spellbinding effect this encounter has on the narrator, as they sing, “God, I fell into your spell, on the rite of God we fell, you were blush and I laid bare, you had me howling.” Here, the use of religious imagery juxtaposed with their own desires signifies the intensity of their connection.

The second verse delves deeper into the narrator’s emotions, as they repeat the words “who, oh, oh, blush” and “golden siren.” These lines suggest a fascination and infatuation with the subject of their desire. The mention of “exposing” and “laying weakness down on the floor in the backseat” further evokes a sense of surrender and vulnerability. The chorus is then repeated, emphasizing the transformative power of this encounter, as the narrator declares, “You had me howling, caught, I swam into your skin, on the night you led me under your sin.”

The bridge of the song features the repetition of “who, oh, oh, blush,” intensifying the emotional turmoil experienced by the narrator. It reflects their longing and desire for this connection to continue. The song comes to a close with the outro, where the narrator acknowledges being under the spell of their desire. The repetition of “under your spell” emphasizes the ultimate surrender they experienced. The final lines, however, remain ambiguous, leaving room for interpretation.

Personally, “Howling” by Cathedrals holds a special significance for me. The haunting melody combined with the powerful lyrics creates an atmosphere of longing and desire that resonates deeply. The song speaks to the intensity of connections and the vulnerability that comes with allowing oneself to be consumed by desire.

I recall a time when I first heard this song during a late-night drive. The combination of the dark, empty streets and the ethereal quality of the music created a surreal and introspective moment. As the lyrics enveloped me, I found myself reflecting on past encounters and the power they held over me. The raw emotions and longing expressed in “Howling” tapped into a part of me that had been dormant for a while.

This song captures the universal human experience of being captivated by desire and the allure of another person. It explores the inherent vulnerability we feel when we open ourselves up to these intense emotions. The lyrics remind us of the transcendental nature of such encounters, where we can feel as if we are under a spell, howling with a mix of pleasure and pain.

In conclusion, “Howling” by Cathedrals may be shrouded in mystery when it comes to its origins and specific meaning. However, the power of its lyrics and the emotions they evoke cannot be denied. The song serves as an anthem of desire and surrender, reminding us of the complexity and allure of human connections. Whether experiencing it on a late-night drive or within the quiet confines of your room, “Howling” has the power to transport you to a place where vulnerability and passion intertwine.
